Crypto Tax Basics for South Africans (2025)

April 1, 2026

Last updated: Loading...

SARS treats crypto as taxable assets. Every trade, conversion, or disposal can trigger capital gains tax. As a South African crypto trader, you must report accurately. This guide covers the essentials.

Key Principles

  • Crypto ≠ currency — SARS considers it "intangible property" for tax purposes
  • Disposal = taxable event — Selling, swapping, or spending crypto triggers tax
  • Capital gains tax (CGT) — Up to 40% of your gain may be taxed (depending on income bracket)
  • Income tax applies if you're trading as a business (frequent, systematic trading)

When You Owe Tax

✅ Taxable Events

  1. Selling crypto for ZAR — From bid price to exit price
  2. Trading one crypto for another — Imagine you sold the first one for ZAR, then bought the second
  3. Spending crypto — E.g., buying goods/services with BTC; the market value in ZAR at time of spend is taxable
  4. Staking rewards — Treated as income (market value at receipt), then subject to CGT on later disposal
  5. Airdrops — Income at receipt (market value), then CGT when sold
  6. DeFi yield farming — Similar treatment; distribution of new tokens = income

🚫 Non-Taxable Events

  • Holding crypto (no change in value until disposal)
  • Transferring between own wallets (same ownership, no disposal)

How SARS Calculates Capital Gains

Proceeds – what you received (ZAR value at disposal time) Base cost – what you paid (purchase price + fees) Gain = Proceeds – Base cost

Only 40% of your net capital gains are added to your taxable income each year. So effective tax rate: max(0, 40% × Gain × your marginal tax rate)

Record-Keeping Requirements

You must keep records for 5 years:

  • Date of acquisition
  • Date and ZAR value at disposal
  • Amount of crypto bought/sold/traded
  • Purpose of the transaction (personal, investment, business)
  • Wallet addresses involved

Tools for SA Traders

Manual tracking is error-prone. Use:

  • Koinly or CoinLedger — import Luno CSV exports, auto-calc gains
  • Excel sheet — track every trade with ZAR values
  • API logs — our bot can be extended to log every action for tax reporting

Example: Calculating a Taxable Gain

Scenario:

  • Bought 1 ETH @ R50,000 (Jan 2025) + R100 fees = base cost R50,100
  • Swapped 1 ETH for 3500 TRX (Mar 2025) — ETH ZAR value then = R60,000
  • Later sold 3500 TRX for R55,000

Tax events:

  1. ETH→TRX swap: Gain = R60,000 – R50,100 = R9,900
  2. TRX→ZAR sale: Gain = R55,000 – (you'll need base cost of TRX from swap; use R60,000 as starting value for simplicity) → R55,000 – R60,000 = -R5,000 loss

Net capital gain for year = R9,900 – R5,000 = R4,900. Add 40% of that (R1,960) to taxable income, taxed at your marginal rate.

VAT? No.

Crypto-to-crypto trades are VAT-exempt (SARS confirmed). Only the capital gain component is relevant.

Tips to Stay Compliant

  • Use dedicated API keys for your bot and keep logs
  • Export Luno transaction history quarterly (Settings → Export data)
  • Consider forming a PTY and trading as a business (different tax rules)
  • Consult a tax practitioner familiar with crypto

Conclusion

Pay your taxes, sleep well. The penalties for non-compliance far outweigh the tax burden. Track everything meticulously and include crypto gains in your annual tax return.
